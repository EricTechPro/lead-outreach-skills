# Offer framework (value-equation framing)

Use this to pressure-test the offer before it becomes the campaign goal. The point isn't a long
document — it's a *sharp* one. A vague offer produces vague, ignorable outreach.

## The value equation (Hormozi-style) — score the offer in your head

A lead acts when **(Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)** is high.
When the offer feels weak, it's almost always one of these:

| Lever | Ask yourself | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Dream outcome** | Is the win concrete and *theirs*, not yours? | Name the outcome in their words. |
| **Perceived likelihood** | Why would they believe us? | Lead with the most *verifiable* proof. |
| **Time delay** | How fast do they see value? | Promise a fast, small first win. |
| **Effort** | What do they have to do? | Make the ask one low-effort step (a 15-min call, a reply). |

You don't write the equation into `campaign.md`. You use it to choose the **hook** (highest
perceived-likelihood asset) and the **ask** (lowest-effort next step).

## The goal is flexible — pick ONE primary win-condition

Outreach is not only selling. The goal shapes the whole message:

- **Sell** — they buy / book a call. Hook = proof + a fast first win.
- **Partner / collab** — joint project, cross-promo. Hook = complementary audiences, mutual upside.
- **Network** — a relationship, no immediate ask. Hook = genuine specific interest.
- **Guest / podcast swap** — appear on each other's channels. Hook = a concrete, on-topic angle + your reach.
- **Hire / be hired** — Hook = a directly relevant shipped result.
- **Just reach out** — warm, specific, no pitch.

## campaign.md template (keep it under ~40 lines)

```markdown
# Campaign: <name>

## Who we are + what we offer
<One paragraph: who, the lane, the one thing we're known for.>

**Verifiable assets** (every claim has a source — no source, no claim):
- <asset> — <source URL>
- <number/outcome> — <source URL>

## Who we're targeting
- Segment: <e.g. AI-automation YouTubers, 50K–1M subs>
- Size / signal: <how we know they're a fit>
- Their pain we relieve: <the gap our offer fills>
- Where they are: <platform / community>

## The goal + the angle
- **Goal (one win):** <Sell | Partner | Network | Guest swap | Hire | Reach out>
- **The angle (one line every message bends toward):** <the through-line>
- **The ask (lowest-effort next step):** <e.g. "worth a 15-min call?">
```

## The verifiability rule (non-negotiable)
- Numbers and outcomes need a **source URL**. No source → cut it, and say so in one line.
- A number that belongs to a *different* person/company is never ours. Misattributed proof is the
  fastest way to get ignored or blocked.
- Favor assets a prospect can check themselves: a live product page, a repo's star count, a public
  subscriber count, a real bio line.
